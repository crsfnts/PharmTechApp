1. Atorvastatin

Brand name: Lipitor (and generics)
Drug class: HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor (Statin)
Common uses: Hyperlipidemia (LDL reduction), primary and secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease
Common side effects: Myalgia, elevated liver enzymes, GI upset, headache
Dosage forms: Oral tablets (e.g., 10, 20, 40, 80 mg)
Pharmacology: Competitive inhibitor of HMG-CoA reductase in the cholesterol biosynthesis pathway; reduces hepatic cholesterol synthesis and upregulates LDL receptors to increase LDL clearance.

2. Metformin

Brand name: Glucophage (and generics)
Drug class: Biguanide (antihyperglycemic)
Common uses: First-line therapy for type 2 diabetes mellitus; insulin resistance management
Common side effects: GI upset (nausea, diarrhea), metallic taste, B12 deficiency with long-term use; rare lactic acidosis in predisposed patients
Dosage forms: Oral immediate-release tablets, extended-release tablets, oral solution
Pharmacology: Decreases hepatic gluconeogenesis, increases peripheral insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake, and reduces intestinal glucose absorption.

3. Levothyroxine

Brand name: Synthroid, Levoxyl, Tirosint
Drug class: Thyroid hormone replacement (synthetic T4)
Common uses: Hypothyroidism replacement therapy; TSH suppression in certain thyroid cancers or nodules
Common side effects: Symptoms of over-replacement (palpitations, tremor, weight loss, insomnia), rare allergic reactions
Dosage forms: Oral tablets, oral solution, intravenous formulation
Pharmacology: Synthetic thyroxine (T4) converted peripherally to active T3; binds nuclear thyroid hormone receptors to modulate gene expression and increase metabolic rate.

4. Lisinopril

Brand name: Prinivil, Zestril
Drug class: ACE inhibitor

Common side effects: Cough (class effect), hyperkalemia, hypotension, dizziness; rare angioedema
Dosage forms: Oral tablets (2.5–40 mg strengths)
Pharmacology: Inhibits angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), reducing conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, decreasing vasoconstriction and aldosterone secretion.
